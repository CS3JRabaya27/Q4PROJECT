Nuclear Science: Exploring the Use of Nuclear Reactions for Power Generation

Nuclear energy is a powerful and efficient source of electricity generated through nuclear reactions, primarily fission, where atomic nuclei split to release energy. This process takes place in nuclear power plants, where the heat produced from fission is used to generate steam that drives turbines to produce electricity. As a low-carbon energy source, nuclear power contributes to reducing greenhouse gas emissions, making it a key player in the transition towards sustainable energy systems. However, it also poses challenges, including radioactive waste management and concerns about safety and environmental impacts.

Nuclear energy is a form of energy produced through nuclear reactions, primarily used for electricity generation. It is derived from the nucleus of atoms, the core that contains protons and neutrons. There are two primary methods to harness nuclear energy: nuclear fission and nuclear fusion.
