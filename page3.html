What is Nuclear Fusion?

Introduction to Nuclear Fusion

Nuclear fusion is a process where two light atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus, releasing a tremendous amount of energy. This reaction powers the stars, including our sun, and holds great promise for providing a nearly limitless and clean source of energy if harnessed on Earth.

The Science Behind Nuclear Fusion

In nuclear fusion, isotopes of hydrogen such as deuterium and tritium are typically used. When these nuclei collide with enough force, they overcome their electrostatic repulsion and merge to form a helium nucleus and a neutron, releasing energy in the process. This reaction requires extremely high temperatures and pressures, conditions naturally found in stars.

Applications of Nuclear Fusion

While practical applications of nuclear fusion are still in the experimental stage, the potential is enormous. Fusion reactors, if successfully developed, could provide a nearly inexhaustible supply of energy with minimal environmental impact. Projects like ITER are working towards making fusion energy a reality, which could revolutionize how we produce power.
